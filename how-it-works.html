Workflow overview

How MBSRN turns a weak site into a practical SEO plan

MBSRN is an operator-first system. It combines audits, evidence, competitor context, and AI-assisted drafts with human review gates—so nothing reaches production without approval.

The MBSRN workflow in 7 clear steps

  1. 1. Capture operator requirements

    Start with what matters: your business goals, target locations, core services, and any existing systems (e.g., booking tools, CRMs). Operators and admins decide what data to connect and who needs to review changes.

  2. 2. Ingest your current website

    MBSRN scans your existing site—whether it's on GoDaddy or another host—and builds a snapshot of your current pages, structure, and key SEO signals.

  3. 3. Run an SEO audit

    Using your site snapshot, Google Search Console, GA4, and Google Business Profile (when available), MBSRN highlights technical issues, content gaps, and local visibility problems.

  4. 4. Pull in competitor insights

    The system reviews relevant local competitors to see how they show up in search, what services they emphasize, and which content types you may be missing.

  5. 5. Generate recommendations

    AI helps draft a structured set of recommendations: what to fix, why it matters, what evidence supports it, and what outcome you can reasonably expect. Operators refine and approve before anything moves forward.

  6. 6. Create migration-ready drafts

    For sites that need a bigger upgrade, MBSRN can generate draft static pages and navigation based on your requirements and approved recommendations. These are artifacts for review—not live changes.

  7. 7. Approve, publish, and deploy with control

    After human review, admins and operators decide what to publish and where. MBSRN emphasizes safe, trackable workflows; it does not push changes to production without explicit authorization.

What each role does in the workflow

Admins

Admins own the guardrails. They configure environments, approval rules, and connections to analytics and search tools.

  • Set who can trigger audits and migrations.
  • Configure connections to Google Search Console, GA4, and other approved tools.
  • Define publishing workflows and sign-off requirements.

Operators

Operators live in the day-to-day workflow. They interpret results, prioritize work, and decide which changes move forward.

  • Run audits and competitor scans.
  • Review evidence and draft recommendations.
  • Approve or reject migration drafts.

Users & business owners

Users get clarity, not noise. They see summaries, before/after comparisons, and simple next steps.

  • Understand what's changing and why.
  • Focus on outcomes like leads, bookings, or visits.
  • Stay informed without managing every technical detail.

Review gates at every critical step

Nothing in MBSRN skips review. Each stage is designed with a clear “stop and think” moment so you can confirm changes before they impact customers.

  • Audit review: Operators validate findings against business reality.
  • Recommendation review: Items are checked for clarity, impact, and feasibility.
  • Migration draft review: New pages, navigation, and copy are reviewed like any other content project.
  • Publish review: Admins confirm what goes live and where. No automatic production changes.

AI assists with drafting content and recommendations, but humans own the decisions.

Safer than ad-hoc changes

Many businesses rely on quick fixes: a freelancer here, a one-off agency project there. MBSRN gives you a structured alternative:

  • Every change connects back to a recommendation and its evidence.
  • You can explain why a change was made months later.
  • Team members don't need to dig through old email threads to understand decisions.
