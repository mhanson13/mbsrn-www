The MBSRN workflow at a glance

1. Intake & goals

Capture your current website URL, service area, and business goals. Operators and owners define what "better" looks like: more calls, clearer services, improved local visibility, or a full migration.

2. SEO audit

MBSRN ingests your existing site and analyzes key signals:

  • Site structure, internal links, and on-page content
  • Technical issues that can block or confuse search engines
  • Connections to Google Search Console, GA4, and Google Business Profile (when available)

3. Competitor insights

We surface local competitors so you can see how your website compares:

  • Content gaps: pages or topics competitors cover that you do not
  • Local SEO signals: how they present services, locations, and reviews
  • Structure ideas: how they organize service pages, FAQs, and CTAs

4. Recommendations

Based on the audit and competitor context, MBSRN creates actionable recommendations that explain:

  • What to adjust: specific pages, sections, or technical items
  • Why it matters: how it supports visibility, clarity, or conversions
  • Evidence: references to audit findings or competitor examples
  • Expected outcome: what you should see if implemented well

5. Draft content & migration artifacts

AI helps generate draft page copy, layouts, and static-site artifacts based on your requirements. Examples include:

  • Rewritten service pages and clearer navigation
  • New local landing pages for high-value search terms
  • Draft static HTML and CSS ready for your preferred hosting

These are drafts only—operators and owners retain full control over what is edited, accepted, or discarded.

6. Review gates & approvals

Before anything is moved toward production:

  • Operators review each recommendation and draft artifact
  • Changes can be updated, combined, or paused
  • Business owners can review in plain language before signing off

7. Safe publishing workflow

Once approved, changes can move to staging or live environments based on admin-configured publishing targets.

MBSRN is designed to support controlled publishing. It does not push automatic production changes without explicit operator or admin approval.

Who does what: Admins, Operators, and Users.

Admins

  • Set up accounts and permissions
  • Connect Google Search Console, GA4, and other data sources
  • Configure where drafts can be published or exported
  • Define review rules and approval policies

Operators

  • Run audits and competitor reviews
  • Interpret recommendations and adjust language for the business
  • Coordinate with owners on priorities and timing
  • Approve which drafts move forward to staging or production

Users & owners

  • Review findings in clear, non-technical language
  • Provide context about services, pricing, and target customers
  • Approve the final content and migration plan
  • Track which actions have been completed

What MBSRN does—and what it intentionally avoids.

We focus on practical SEO operations and safer website changes. That means being clear about limitations.

  • MBSRN does not guarantee specific search rankings or business outcomes.
  • MBSRN does not replace the need for human review and decision-making.
  • MBSRN avoids automatic production changes without explicit approval.
  • MBSRN avoids exposing internal infrastructure, secrets, or deployment internals in this workflow.
