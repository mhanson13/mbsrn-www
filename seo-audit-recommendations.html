What the SEO audit looks at

The MBSRN audit focuses on the parts of your website that actually affect visibility and conversions for small businesses.

  • Technical signals: basic crawlability, index coverage, and page performance indicators.
  • On-page content: clarity of service pages, headings, titles, and internal links.
  • Local readiness: how well your site supports Google Business Profile and local queries.
  • Analytics alignment: what GA4 and other data say about which pages are pulling their weight.

The output is a structured set of findings—not a vague score.

From findings to recommendations you can act on

Every recommendation in MBSRN aims to answer four questions:

  1. What should we change? A clear, specific action like “Create a dedicated Service Area page for ‘Furnace Repair in 80542.’”
  2. Why does it matter? A short explanation tying the change to search visibility, conversions, or user experience.
  3. What evidence backs it up? Links or references to audit findings, Google Search Console data, GA4 reports, or competitor examples.
  4. What outcome should we expect? A grounded expectation, not a guarantee (e.g., “More visibility for ‘furnace repair near me' queries in your service area.”).

Plain-English summaries

Owners and non-technical staff see the “why” and “so what” without jargon, while operators can still dig into evidence.

Impact and effort

Recommendations include a rough sense of expected impact and implementation effort so you can prioritize realistically.

Grouped into practical workstreams

Instead of a long flat list, items can be grouped into themes like “Local SEO foundations,” “Content improvements,” or “Technical fixes.”

Competitor insights without copying

MBSRN reviews comparable local competitors to help you understand where you're strong, where you're thin, and where your site can stand out.

  • Identify missing service pages or content types.
  • See how competitors position pricing, guarantees, and service areas.
  • Spot opportunities in reviews, FAQs, and local landing pages.

Insights are used to shape your plan—not to duplicate someone else's website.
